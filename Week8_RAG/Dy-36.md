# Introduction to RAG

Large Language Models (LLMs) such as ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, and Llama are powerful, but they have limitations:

* They may not know the latest information.
* They cannot access private company documents by default.
* They can generate hallucinations (incorrect answers).

To solve these problems, modern AI systems use **Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)**.

## What is RAG?

RAG stands for **Retrieval-Augmented Generation**.

It combines two processes:

### 1. Retrieval

Finding relevant information from external sources.

### 2. Generation

Using an LLM to generate a response based on the retrieved information.

---

# Why Do We Need RAG?

## Without RAG

```text
Question
   ↓
LLM
   ↓
Answer
```

Problems:

* Hallucinations
* Outdated information
* No access to private knowledge

---

## With RAG

```text
Question
   ↓
Retrieve Information
   ↓
Provide Context
   ↓
LLM
   ↓
Answer
```

Benefits:

* Improved accuracy
* Reduced hallucinations
* Up-to-date information
* Access to private company data

# RAG Architecture

A RAG system consists of two major phases:

## Phase 1: Indexing (Preparation)

Preparing documents before users ask questions.

## Phase 2: Retrieval + Generation

Answering user queries using stored knowledge.

# Components of RAG Architecture

## Documents

Knowledge sources such as:

* PDFs
* Websites
* Databases
* Word Documents
* Research Papers

---

## Chunking

Large documents are divided into smaller sections called chunks.

Example:

```text
100-Page PDF
```

becomes:

```text
Chunk 1
Chunk 2
Chunk 3
Chunk 4
```

---

## Embeddings

Each chunk is converted into a vector representation.

Example:

```text
Leave Policy
```

↓

```text
[0.45, 0.78, -0.32, ...]
```

---

## Vector Database

Stores embeddings for efficient searching.

Popular Vector Databases:

* FAISS
* Pinecone
* ChromaDB
* Weaviate
* Milvus

---

## Retrieval

Finds the most relevant chunks based on the user's question.

---

## LLM

Uses retrieved context to generate an accurate answer.

---

# Retrieval Pipeline

The retrieval pipeline is the process used to find relevant information before generating a response.

---

## Step 1: User Query

Example:

```text
How many casual leaves are allowed?
```

---

## Step 2: Query Embedding

Convert the query into a vector.

```text
Query
   ↓
Embedding Model
   ↓
Vector
```

---

## Step 3: Similarity Search

Search the vector database for similar embeddings.

---

## Step 4: Retrieve Relevant Chunks

Example:

```text
Employees are entitled to 12 casual leaves annually.
```

---

## Step 5: Send Context to LLM

```text
Context + Question
      ↓
     LLM
```

---

## Step 6: Generate Answer

```text
Employees can take 12 casual leaves per year.
```

---

# Chunking Strategies

Chunking is one of the most important parts of a RAG system.

Poor chunking leads to poor retrieval results.

---

## What is Chunking?

Breaking large documents into smaller pieces.

Example:

Original Document:

```text
Artificial Intelligence is transforming healthcare.
AI helps doctors diagnose diseases.
Machine learning improves predictions.
```

Chunks:

```text
Chunk 1:
Artificial Intelligence is transforming healthcare.

Chunk 2:
AI helps doctors diagnose diseases.

Chunk 3:
Machine learning improves predictions.
```

---

## Why Chunking?

* Makes retrieval more accurate
* Improves embedding quality
* Handles large documents efficiently

---

# Types of Chunking Strategies

## 1. Fixed-Size Chunking

Split documents into equal-sized chunks.

Example:

```text
500 words per chunk
```

### Advantages

* Easy implementation

### Disadvantages

* May break important context

---

## 2. Sentence-Based Chunking

Split based on sentence boundaries.

### Advantages

* Natural structure

### Disadvantages

* Uneven chunk sizes

---

## 3. Paragraph-Based Chunking

Split based on paragraphs.

### Advantages

* Preserves context better

### Disadvantages

* Some chunks may be too large

---

## 4. Recursive Chunking

Most widely used strategy.

Process:

```text
Paragraph
   ↓
Sentence
   ↓
Words
```

The document is repeatedly split until the desired size is achieved.

### Used By

* LangChain
* LlamaIndex

---

## 5. Overlapping Chunking

Some content appears in multiple chunks.

Example:

```text
Chunk 1: Words 1–100

Chunk 2: Words 80–180
```

Words 80–100 appear in both chunks.

### Benefits

* Preserves context
* Improves retrieval quality

---

# Re-Ranking

A production-level technique used to improve retrieval quality.

---

## The Problem

Similarity search may retrieve:

```text
Chunk A
Chunk B
Chunk C
Chunk D
Chunk E
```

Not all chunks are equally useful.

---

## Example

Question:

```text
What is machine learning?
```

Retrieved Results:

```text
Chunk A → Highly Relevant
Chunk B → Relevant
Chunk C → Slightly Relevant
Chunk D → Less Relevant
Chunk E → Irrelevant
```

---

## What is Re-Ranking?

Re-ranking reorders retrieved results based on relevance.

### Before Re-Ranking

```text
A
B
C
D
E
```

### After Re-Ranking

```text
A
B
C
```

Only the best results are sent to the LLM.

---

## Benefits

* Better answer quality
* Reduced noise
* More relevant context

---

## Re-Ranking Workflow

```text
User Query
      │
      ▼
Vector Search
      │
      ▼
Top 20 Results
      │
      ▼
Re-Ranker
      │
      ▼
Top 5 Results
      │
      ▼
LLM
```

---

## Popular Re-Ranking Models

* BGE Reranker
* Cohere Rerank
* Cross Encoder Models

# Hybrid Search

One of the most widely used retrieval techniques in industry.

---

## Problem with Vector Search

Vector search focuses on meaning.

Example:

```text
Error Code: ABC123
```

Query:

```text
ABC123
```

Semantic search may not retrieve the exact match.

---

## Keyword Search

Finds exact matches.

Search:

```text
ABC123
```

Returns:

```text
ABC123
```

---

## Vector Search

Finds semantic matches.

Search:

```text
Car
```

Can retrieve:

```text
Vehicle
Automobile
SUV
Sedan
```

---

# What is Hybrid Search?

Hybrid Search combines:

```text
Keyword Search
      +
Vector Search
```

This provides both exact matching and semantic understanding.

---

# Hybrid Search Architecture

```text
User Query
      │
 ┌────┴────┐
 │         │
 ▼         ▼

Keyword   Vector
Search    Search

 │         │
 └────┬────┘
      ▼

Combine Results
      │
      ▼

Re-Rank Results
      │
      ▼

LLM
      │
      ▼

Answer
```
